With some additional information, we can turn those two-dimensional box annotations into full, three-dimensional boxes, with height, width, depth, rotation, and relative positioning info. A little background



Alegion transforms unstructured data into high quality, model-ready training data through a team of data experts, AI-powered annotation services, and a skilled global workforce. We find ourselves at an intersection of AI and human work. As a result, the largest userbase is in people who train the data for AI to work on. Our clients bring us data in the form of images, video etc, and want it labelled to use for various purposes. For example, in this grocery store scene, our platform can be used to label a person by drawing a bounding box around it, and then choosing a subclassification, such an associated hand. This labeled data can then be used by the clients for other purposes such as analysing performance patterns.

Until recently, the only way data could be annotated was using bounding bozes and polygons, but the existing tools fail to capture data that is now readily available in images and videos: a third dimension.



Enter: Cuboid

A cuboid is a 3-D shape with sides that are rectangular. Cuboids have six faces, and twelve edges. Cuboid Annotation is the task of labeling objects in 2D images with cuboids. 3D cuboids are similar to bounding boxes but provide additional depth information about the object. Thus, with 3D cuboids you can get a 3D representation of the object, allowing systems to distinguish features like volume and position in a 3D space.



Use cases

Autonomous Autonomous Vehicle

Cuboid Annotation helps autonomous vehicles to understand the real-world environment. It mainly aids in detecting vehicle movement and its dimension for autonomous vehicles. It helps the self-driving cars to measure the distance of each obstacle from the vehicle and calculate the spacing.




Identifying Indoor Objects

You can build the model perception for detecting indoor objects using the 3D cuboid annotated images. This helps to train your Computer Vision models to have in-depth object detection capabilities. Cuboid Annotation helps to identify indoor objects like couch, table and other furniture with precision and best quality.




Training Robots

Cuboid Annotation can be effectively used for training robots that are deployed in different industries such as automotive, warehousing, etc. It helps to create better perception models that enable the robots to work continuously without the need for human interference. The 3D Cuboid Annotation of images captured from 2D cameras, powers perception of the robots and drone imagery which have applications in various fields.

Concept exploration: Degrees of freedom

Vertices

These give 8 degrees of freedom, and practically lands a freeform cuboid

  • Extreme flexibility

  • Hard for users to interpret

  • Too complex to build










Faces

Offers 6 degrees of freedom

  • Medium flexibiilty

  • Simple to understand

  • Medium difficulty of implementation










Edges

Offers 12 degrees of freedom

  • Medium flexibility

  • Difficult to interpret

The Alegion cuboid



The cuboid has all the properties of a cuboid, but to manage complexity we only expose 2 faces of the cuboid to the annotator: The first face: It has all the properties of the bounding box; The second face: This face is dropped by hovering either on the right or the left of the first face. It’s length is determined by as far as the annotator would go. The adjoining edge is fixed, but the 2 vertices of the opposite face are free.



There are 2 kinds of edits possible in the Alegion cuboid: The first face has all the edits possible to a bounding box. The 2 vertices of the free face are freely editable. This is indicated to the end user in the following corresponding ways: The edges of the first face have “frame corners” in a different color. The 2 free vertices are circled in a different color.
